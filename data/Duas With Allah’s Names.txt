Names Explained
1. Ya Rahman (The Most Merciful)
* Derived from the root R-H-M, meaning womb, indicating a nurturing, deeply maternal compassion.
* Refers to Allah's universal mercy that encompasses all creation, regardless of their belief.
* Reminds believers that His boundless grace sustains all earthly existence and life.
2. Ya Wahid (The One)
* Rooted in W-H-D, meaning absolute uniqueness and indivisibility.
* Signifies that Allah is utterly alone in His essence, without partners or equals.
* Anchors the core Islamic principle of Tawhid, directing all pure devotion solely to Him.
3. Ya Witr (The Singular)
* Refers to that which is odd-numbered or strictly singular.
* Emphasizes that Allah is unmatched, independent, and completely unparalleled.
* Spiritually encourages believers to seek Him in solitude, matching His uniqueness with undivided worship.
4. Ya Hadi (The Guide)
* From H-D-Y, meaning to guide, direct, or show the way gently.
* Denotes the One who provides innate physical guidance to creation and spiritual guidance to hearts.
* Reassures believers that true direction through life's darkness comes strictly from His light.
5. Ya Rashid (The Guide to the Right Path)
* Root R-Sh-D indicates moving in the right direction with supreme wisdom.
* Represents the Unerring Director who leads creation to what is ultimately beneficial.
* Inspires believers to align their natural instincts with righteousness and truth.
6. Ya Nur (The Light)
* Means the literal and spiritual Light that illuminates the heavens and the earth.
* Signifies the One who brings hearts out of the darkness of ignorance and doubt.
* Reminds us that faith itself is a divine light granted to those seeking clarity.
7. Ya Mubin (The Evident)
* From B-Y-N, meaning to be clear, manifest, and perfectly distinct.
* The One who makes the truth obvious and clears away all falsehood and ambiguity.
* Encourages believers to look for His clear signs in the universe and within themselves.
8. Ya Waliyy (The Protecting Friend)
* Root W-L-Y means to be close, to manage, and to lovingly protect.
* Denotes Allah as the ultimate guardian and ally of the believers.
* Provides profound comfort that those who trust Him are never truly alone or undefended.
9. Ya Rafeeq (The Gentle Companion)
* From R-F-Q, denoting ultimate gentleness, kindness, and compassionate companionship.
* Highlights that Allah acts with softness in His decrees and loves gentleness in His servants.
* Comforts the lonely by assuring them of His subtle, tender presence in their lives.
10. Ya Rafi' (The Exalter)
* Root R-F-' means to lift up, elevate, or raise in status.
* The One who honors the righteous by raising their ranks in this life and the next.
* Teaches that true elevation comes only through sincere humility before Him.
11. Ya Mawla (The Protector)
* Shares the root W-L-Y, emphasizing supreme lordship, mastery, and protective care.
* Indicates the ultimate Patron who takes full charge of our affairs when we surrender.
* Assures the believer that having Allah as a protector makes all worldly fears insignificant.
12. Ya Nasir (The Helper)
* From N-S-R, meaning to grant support, aid, and ultimate victory.
* The One who assists the oppressed and grants triumphs that no earthly power can stop.
* Reminds believers that true victory is entirely dependent on His unseen divine support.
13. Ya Sayyid (The Master)
* Means the ultimate Lord and Chief possessing absolute authority over all.
* Denotes that He is the only One truly worthy of absolute, unconditional servitude.
* Strips away human arrogance, reminding us that true dignity lies in being His servant.
14. Ya Malik (The King)
* Root M-L-K indicates supreme sovereignty, ownership, and dominion.
* The absolute King who commands the universe and to whom all rises and falls belong.
* Cultivates deep gratitude by reminding us that everything we "own" is merely loaned by Him.
15. Maliki Yawmid-Deen (Owner of the Day of Judgment)
* Specifically designates Allah as the sole authority on the final Day of Reckoning.
* Highlights that on that Day, all earthly kingships will completely dissolve before Him.
* Urges believers to settle their debts and live with intense accountability for the Hereafter.
16. Al-Malik (The Sovereign King)
* Emphasizes the inescapability of His absolute, eternal rule over all creation.
* Reminds humanity that His sovereignty is perfect, needing no validation from His subjects.
* Humbles the rebellious heart, fostering complete contentment under His divine decree.
17. Ya Jalil (The Majestic)
* From J-L-L, meaning to be supreme, magnificent, and profoundly great.
* The One whose immense glory inspires overwhelming awe and deep reverence.
* Refines the believer's character by teaching them to stand small, yet dignified, before His majesty.
18. Ya Khaliq (The Creator)
* Root Kh-L-Q means to measure, proportion, and bring into existence from nothingness.
* Signifies the deliberate, purposeful fashioning of every single atom in the universe.
* Instills profound wonder, reminding us that every breath is an intentional act of His creation.
19. Ya Badi' (The Originator)
* From B-D-', meaning to invent or create completely without any prior model or precedent.
* The One who uniquely designs the universe and flawlessly repairs what is broken.
* Gives hope that He can forge entirely new, beautiful beginnings out of dead ends.
20. Ya Musawwir (The Fashioner)
* Root S-W-R means to form, shape, or give distinct features.
* The One who individually sculpts our inner traits and outer appearances in the womb.
* Encourages believers to seek inner beauty (character) that matches His perfect outer creation.
21. Ya Mubdi' (The Beginner)
* Means the One who initiates all existence and originates life effortlessly.
* Represents the continuous power to create fresh openings and realities for His servants.
* Comforts those who feel stuck, assuring them that He can always begin things anew.
22. Ya Jabbar (The Restorer / Mender / Compeller)
* From J-B-R, which beautifully means both to forcefully compel and to restore a broken bone.
* The One who irresistibly mends shattered hearts and broken lives with absolute power.
* Guarantees that His divine will overcomes all tyranny while tenderly healing the oppressed.
23. Ya Samad (The Self-Sufficient)
* Means the ultimate, solid foundation upon whom all of existence entirely depends.
* The One who requires no sustenance, rest, or assistance from anyone or anything.
* Cures spiritual emptiness by teaching that wholeness is only found in depending on Him.
24. Ya Haqq (The Truth)
* Root H-Q-Q denotes absolute reality, rightness, and that which is undeniable.
* The unshakeable Reality that exposes and collapses all earthly lies and deceptions.
* Serves as the ultimate anchor, keeping the believer grounded in eternal, enduring truth.
25. Ya Wasi' (The All-Embracing)
* From W-S-', meaning vast, expansive, and limitless in capacity.
* Describes His boundless knowledge, endless mercy, and limitless provision.
* Expands the human heart to hold more patience, vision, and gratitude than we thought possible.
26. Ya Ghaffar (The All-Forgiving)
* Root Gh-F-R means to continuously cover, protect, and pardon.
* The One who forgives repeatedly, no matter how many times a servant falters.
* Eradicates despair, ensuring that shame never blocks the path of returning to His mercy.
27. Ya Tawwab (The Acceptor of Repentance)
* From T-W-B, meaning to return repeatedly.
* The One who constantly turns toward His servants in mercy so they can turn to Him in repentance.
* Makes seeking forgiveness a joyful, constant companion rather than a burdensome chore.
28. Ya Wadud (The Most Loving)
* Root W-D-D signifies an active, expressive, and deeply affectionate love.
* Represents the One who continuously shows love through abundant, often unnoticed blessings.
* Inspires believers to find immense sweetness in His love, even amidst life's difficult tests.
29. Ya Muhsin (The Benefactor)
* From H-S-N, meaning ultimate beauty, excellence, and perfection in action.
* The One who perfects everything He touches and gives with absolute grace.
* Motivates believers to perform deeds with Ihsan (excellence) as a reflection of His perfection.
30. Ya Shakur (The Most Appreciative)
* Root Sh-K-R means to acknowledge, multiply, and immensely reward.
* The One who abundantly rewards even the smallest, most broken efforts of His servants.
* Reminds us that the very ability to be grateful is itself a profound gift from Him.
31. Ya Sami' (The All-Hearing)
* Means the One who hears every whisper, thought, and loud declaration flawlessly.
* Listens intimately to the unspoken prayers hidden deep within the chest.
* Assures the believer that their calls are never ignored, unheard, or forgotten.
32. Ya Qarib (The Near One)
* Root Q-R-B denotes physical or spiritual proximity and intimate closeness.
* The One who is nearer to us than our own jugular vein, fully aware of our internal states.
* Provides intense comfort, cutting through the noise and isolation of the external world.
33. Ya Mujib (The Responsive)
* From J-W-B, meaning to answer, reply, and actively respond.
* The One who fulfills needs with perfect wisdom, often giving what is best rather than what is asked.
* Protects the heart from ever doubting that an answer is on its way.
34. Ya Mu'min (The Giver of Faith)
* Root A-M-N signifies safety, security, and the granting of absolute trust.
* The source of unshakeable faith that secures the heart against worldly fears and anxieties.
* Guards the soul from the whispers of doubt, making trust in Him heavier than any worry.
35. Ya Raqib (The Watchful)
* From R-Q-B, meaning to observe closely, guard diligently, and remain vigilant.
* The ever-watchful Guardian who never sleeps, protecting us in our most vulnerable moments.
* Cultivates Taqwa (mindfulness), knowing we are constantly beneath His protective gaze.
36. Ya Wakil (The Trustee)
* Root W-K-L means to entrust a matter entirely to a capable manager.
* The ultimate Disposer of Affairs who perfectly handles whatever we release into His control.
* Relieves the immense burden of worldly anxiety by taking charge of what we cannot carry.
37. Ya Basir (The All-Seeing)
* Means the One who completely sees the seen and the unseen, the physical and the spiritual.
* Witnesses every hidden tear, unnoticed effort, and silent act of good.
* Brings deep comfort to those who feel invisible or unappreciated by the rest of the world.
38. Ya Shahid (The Witness)
* From Sh-H-D, meaning to testify, be present, and observe with absolute awareness.
* The ultimate Witness to the purity of our intentions when others misjudge us.
* Instills a consciousness that drives the believer to live and die truthfully under His sight.
39. Ya Hafiz (The Preserver)
* Root H-F-Z means to guard, protect, and maintain carefully.
* The One who preserves our physical well-being, our loved ones, and most importantly, our faith.
* Acts as our vital shield, safeguarding the blessings we frequently forget to thank Him for.
40. Ya Sattar (The Concealer of Sins)
* From S-T-R, meaning to drape, cover, or hide completely.
* The One who graciously veils our private flaws, sins, and shameful acts from public disgrace.
* Allows repentance to remain a dignified, private affair between the servant and the Lord.
41. Ya Subhan (The Most Glorified)
* Means the One who is utterly transcendent, perfect, and completely free of any defect.
* Reminds us that He is entirely untouched by the chaos, pain, and heaviness of the worldly life.
* Pulls the believer out of spiritual darkness through the sheer power of His absolute perfection.
42. Ya Quddus (The Pure)
* Root Q-D-S denotes absolute holiness, purity, and flawless sanctity.
* The One who is entirely free from the flaws and limitations of human nature.
* Purifies the believer's heart from pride, resentment, and anything that clouds the soul.
43. Ya Salam (The Source of Peace)

Duaas For Each Name
1. Ya Rahman (The Most Merciful)
Ya Rahman, from Your throne You wrote mercy upon Yourself. You were merciful before there was anyone to receive mercy. Embrace every part of me with that mercy—my past with its scars, my present with its needs, my future with its uncertainties. Let me taste the mercy You keep for those who return. Reward me with the mercy I have yet to know, and raise me to the highest place in Paradise despite my lows. Cover me from storms I don't see coming. Mend me before I break. Spare me from trials of every kind. Let Your mercy reach me in ways I'll only understand when I finally meet You.
2. Ya Wahid (The One)
Ya Wahid, the One my heart was created to seek. Pull me away from every false worship, from anything that divides my devotion, until nothing fills my longing but You. Unify my pursuits into a single direction, and when the world pulls me apart, center me in Your oneness. Open my heart to see that nothing compares, so that I'm never overly impressed by anyone but You, and I come to love Your names and attributes in a way that frees me from all others but You.
3. Ya Witr (The Singular)
Ya Witr, when I stand alone at the close of the night, let my solitude remind me of Your singular majesty. Make my heart distinct by its devotion, and make me one of Your rarest servants.
4. Ya Hadi (The Guide)
Ya Hadi, guide me when my heart forgets the way. Pull me back from every turn that leads me astray. Let Your guidance feel closer than the doubts in my chest.
5. Ya Rashid (The Guide to the Right Path)
Ya Rashid, teach me to see truth instinctively. Make faith beloved to me, make righteousness feel natural, and let sin feel heavy on my chest and hated by my every breath. Inspire me with wisdom that protects me even from myself.
6. Ya Nur (The Light)
Ya Nur, light every darkness I carried or carry within. Let Your light pour into my heart and onto all my other limbs until You make me a light, and everything I see reminds me of You.
7. Ya Mubin (The Evident)
Ya Mubin, make Your signs clear in what surrounds me and what is within me. Reveal the lessons hidden in every trial. Let me never lose sight of Your signs again.
8. Ya Waliyy (The Protecting Friend)
Ya Waliyy, be my closest friend when the world drifts away. Guard me with the grip that never slips. Keep me firm on the ground of Your goodness. Make my faith steady when my heart trembles. Let me love what brings me to Your stability, and make me patient with what keeps me on its shore.
9. Ya Rafeeq (The Gentle Companion)
Ya Rafeeq, be tender with my soul as You unfold Your plan. Soften the path without removing its purpose. Make me gentle with others as You've been gentle with me. And when I am lonely, fill that space with Your company.
10. Ya Rafi' (The Exalter)
Ya Rafi', raise me the way You raise every seed from the soil. Shape my growth with all You continue to give. Let every season You place me in become a lesson in Your care.
11. Ya Mawla (The Protector)
Ya Mawla, be my protector when I have no one else to stand up for me. Take charge of my affairs when I lose control of them. Hold me near when fear surrounds me, and make Your guardianship always enough for me.
12. Ya Nasir (The Helper)
Ya Nasir, help me in every battle I cannot see. Grant me triumph without arrogance, and victory only in a way that brings me closer to You.
13. Ya Sayyid (The Master)
Ya Sayyid, teach my heart that true honor is to only serve You. Let my dignity be in humility, my leadership in obedience, and my freedom in surrender.
14. Ya Malik (The King)
Ya Malik, You are the King of everything I know and hold. Remind me that nothing I hold is truly mine, and that every rise and fall belongs to Your decree. Let my gratitude expand with every breath You lend to me.
15. Maliki Yawmid-Deen (Owner of the Day of Judgment)
Maliki Yawmid-Deen, the Owner of the Day when debts are paid, teach me to settle my dues before I meet You. Keep me honest with what I owe, and hopeful in what You forgive.
16. Al-Malik (The Sovereign King)
Ya Malik, the King whose command no one can escape, let Your sovereignty humble my pride. Make me content under Your rule, and protect me from a rebellious heart that would cause me to fall out of Your favor.
17. Ya Jalil (The Majestic)
Ya Jalil, fill my heart with reverence that draws me near, not fear that drives me away. Let my awe of You refine me until I stand before You small but never disgraced.
18. Ya Khaliq (The Creator)
Ya Khaliq, You brought me out of nothingness into being. Remind me that my existence itself is a mercy, that every breath, every cell, and every chance is a deliberate act of Your creation.
19. Ya Badi' (The Originator)
Ya Badi', the One who perfects what He produces, repair what I've broken within myself. Make me whole again with the same precision by which You fashioned all of Your creation.
20. Ya Musawwir (The Fashioner)
Ya Musawwir, You shaped me in the unseen, gave me form, face, and story. Beautify my character as You've beautified my features. Let what You see within me be more pleasing than what others see outside of me.
21. Ya Mubdi' (The Beginner)
Ya Mubdi', keep creating new openings when I run out of ways. Create for me new beginnings when I rush to my end, and new realities when I feel doomed by my sins.
22. Ya Jabbar (The Restorer / Mender)
Ya Jabbar, remake me with patience when I reach my limits, faith when I'm afraid, and hope when I can't imagine a way forward.
23. Ya Samad (The Self-Sufficient)
Ya Samad, the One who needs nothing while everything needs You, make me whole by depending only on You. Fill the hollowness inside of me until no desire within me competes with Your glory.
24. Ya Haqq (The Truth)
Ya Haqq, make Your truth the anchor of my heart and my guiding path to Your light. Let every lie I chase collapse before Your reality, and every truth I seek lead me to Your next promise. Keep me grounded in what endures, and turn my eyes away from what deceives.
25. Ya Wasi' (The All-Embracing)
Ya Wasi', expand my heart to hold more gratitude, my patience to endure with more grace, and my vision to see beyond my limits.
26. Ya Ghaffar (The All-Forgiving)
Ya Ghaffar, forgive me for every single sin I've ever put forth. Cover me in this life and the next, and don't let shame keep me from returning to You. For every time I fall and rise again, meet me with another chance. Let my heart grow softer with every repentance, and my view of the door of Your mercy only grow wider, for You have never tired of forgiving me despite my constant sins. Forgive me for what I know and what I don't, what I've done and what I still may do, and turn the stains of my past into light on my scrolls. Reach me with Your forgiveness even in the holes that I've dug for myself.
27. Ya Tawwab (The Acceptor of Repentance)
Ya Tawwab, turn towards me so that I may turn back to You, and accept me every time I return. Make repentance my constant companion and joy, and my reward in finding You again.
28. Ya Wadud (The Most Loving)
Ya Wadud, love me even after I falter, and let Your love reach me through every mercy I overlook. Teach me to love You more than what You give, and to find sweetness in every test that draws me closer to Your love. Announce Your love for me in the heavens until I hear that announcement myself in Paradise. Surround me with it in everything around me, and let me feel it deep inside myself.
29. Ya Muhsin (The Benefactor)
Ya Muhsin, You perfect everything You touch. Make me one of those who do good because You do good. Then let my deeds of excellence to others be a reflection of Your unmatched perfection.
30. Ya Shakur (The Most Appreciative)
Ya Shakur, for every small effort I make, reward me beyond measure. Let me never grow tired of thanking You, and never forget that gratitude itself is a gift from You. Accept from me what is broken and multiply it until it becomes whole.
31. Ya Sami' (The All-Hearing)
Ya Sami', You hear every sound before it's spoken. Inspire in me the words that are pleasing to You. Make sincere the dua that I utter between breaths, and even the ones I make loud alongside others. Let me never doubt that You are listening, and never tire of my calling.
32. Ya Qarib (The Near One)
Ya Qarib, You are nearer than every thought I hide. Let me feel Your nearness in the stillness of my prayer. Let the clarity and comfort of Your closeness cut through all the noise outside, and forgive me for what should have never made it inside.
33. Ya Mujib (The Responsive)
Ya Mujib, answer not only what I ask, but what I mean. Give me what I would have prayed for if I knew what was best for me. When I raise my hands to You, fill them only with what draws me closer to You, and protect me from ever believing that an answer is not possible or questioning You.
34. Ya Mu'min (The Giver of Faith)
Ya Mu'min, grant me faith that no fear can shake. Secure my heart in Your promise, and guard my soul from every whisper that breeds doubt. Let my trust in You be heavier than any worry that visits me.
35. Ya Raqib (The Watchful)
Ya Raqib, the Watchful One who never sleeps, watch over me in the moments that no one else can, and be my shield from even what I see but cannot change. Preserve my faith through trial and time. Guard my loved ones when they are far away from me. Keep safe the blessings I forgot to thank You for, and protect me, for without You I cannot protect myself.
36. Ya Wakil (The Trustee)
Ya Wakil, I entrust to You my every matter—what I seemingly can handle, and that which is obviously beyond my control. Take charge of what confuses me, and carry what I cannot hold. Make Your plan more beloved to me than even my own, for You are enough for me no matter what else I own.
37. Ya Basir (The All-Seeing)
Ya Basir, You see what no one else sees—every tear I hide, every effort unnoticed, every quiet of good. Let me find comfort in being seen by You when I feel invisible to the rest of the world.
38. Ya Shahid (The Witness)
Ya Shahid, be my witness when my intentions are pure, and when they falter, let my fear of You set them aright. Let me live conscious that You are always truly watching, and die as one who lived truthfully under Your sight.
39. Ya Hafiz (The Preserver)
Ya Hafiz, watch over my heart when it drifts, and over my thoughts before they turn to deeds. Make me mindful of You in secret and in public, until awareness of You becomes my protection from slip.
40. Ya Sattar (The Concealer of Sins)
Ya Sattar, cover my faults with the same grace You always have. Hide my flaws even as You heal them. Let my repentance be quiet and my shame sincere, and my forgiveness made complete beneath Your protective veil.
41. Ya Subhan (The Most Glorified)
Ya Subhan, how perfect You are beyond everything I can imagine. When the world feels loud and heavy, remind me that You are untouched by its chaos. Let my glory and praise of You rise above the darkness even if I drown myself in it, and pull me out of it by Your perfection even after I dragged myself down.
42. Ya Quddus (The Pure)
Ya Quddus, purify me inside and out. Cleanse my worship of pride, my heart of resentment, my soul of anything that clouds its way back to You, and make me worthy of the nearness I seek to You.
43. Ya Salam (The Source of Peace)
Ya Salam, send peace into every storm within my chest. Let Your calm settle where fear once lived. Make me a source of peace for others until I meet You in the Home of Peace and get to hear from You Yourself.
44. Ya Ghani (The Self-Sufficient / Free of Need)
Ya Ghani, You are free of all need while I am always in need of You. Let me never beg from those as poor as me. Make me rich through contentment, and fill my heart with gratitude instead of greed.
45. Ya Hamid (The Praiseworthy)
Ya Hamid, You are praised whether or not I remember to say it. Let my praise echo in hardship as it does in ease, and let every breath I take be a quiet thank you.
46. Ya Fattaah (The Opener)
Ya Fattaah, open doors I cannot find and hearts that I cannot reach. Open paths to good where I only see walls, and open my chest to trust whatever You've decreed. Let every opening You grant me lead me back to You.
47. Ya Muqaddim / Ya Mu'akhkhir (The Advancer and Delayer)
Ya Muqaddim, Ya Mu'akhkhir, when You withhold, let me not lose hope. And when You expand, let me not lose humility. Tighten my path only to redirect me to Yours, and widen it in ways that only increase my gratitude to You. Teach me to find You in both the squeeze and the release, and to love You both in the stillness and the flow. When You bring me forward, make me worthy of the moment. And when You hold me back, make me patient with the waiting. Advance me only when the step will please You, and delay me only when the pause will protect me. Wherever I stand in Your decree, let me always feel content that I am never misplaced.
48. Ya Alim (The All-Knowing)
Ya Alim, You know what I hide and what I show, what I've done and what I will do. Let what You know of me be better than what people know of me, and forgive me for what only You know.
49. Ya Hakeem (The Wise)
Ya Hakeem, Your wisdom surrounds what I cannot understand. When I question the purpose, remind me of Your perfection. Grant me the patience to trust Your timing, and the insight to see the beauty unfolding in Your decree.
50. Ya Latif (The Subtle One)
Ya Latif, work gently through the details of my life. Send kindnesses that I may not notice until I look back, but can still feel at every moment. Let Your subtle care reach the corners of my heart that no words or people could ever touch.
51. Ya Khabir (The All-Aware)
Ya Khabir, You are fully aware of what's within me—my fears, my flaws, and my hidden intentions. Purify what You see inside of me until it is pleasing to You, and let nothing inside me here contradict what will be produced when I stand before You.
52. Ya Qahhar (The Subduer)
Ya Qahhar, the One who subdues all false powers, subdue every tyrant who raises a hand against the innocent. Break the arrogance of those who spill the blood of Your beloved servants. Let their cruelty be crushed by Your justice. Subdue also the arrogance within me so that I never resemble the oppressor in any form.
53. Ya Jabbar (The Compeller / Mender)
Ya Jabbar, mend what their hands have broken. Rebuild the lands they've shattered, and heal the hearts that tremble for You under their bombs and their boots. Set right the fractures in our Ummah, and make every wound a doorway back to You. When You allow us to break, let it only be to rebuild us stronger. And when You heal us, let the healing only bring us nearer.
54. Ya Aziz (The Almighty)
Ya Aziz, You are too glorious for injustice to prevail, too mighty for oppression to last. Before You, show the tyrants that no throne stands forever, and honor the humble who still call Your name even beneath the rubble. Clothe our people in dignity when the world strips them bare, raise them in rank, and protect them from despair.
55. Ya Qadir (The All-Powerful)
Ya Qadir, nothing is beyond Your power as You part the seas and revive the dead. Lift our people from beneath the weight of this world. Show us that no injustice is too deep for You to overturn, and no despair too dark for You to light again. Every decree rests in Your command. Only You are able to turn tears into triumph and rubble into resurrection. Your power is perfect, Your execution without flaw. Let those who boast of might see how small they really are. Crush the arrogance that claims control over Your creation, and remind us that even the most helpless among us are upheld by You alone. Grant us strength without cruelty, faith without fatigue, and victory that only humbles us before You until we dwell in that place where our oppressors can no longer reach us, and only those You love stand before You.
56. Ya Adl / Ya Muqsit (The Just)
Ya Adl, Oh You whose balance never tips unfairly, when the world feels rigged for cruelty, remind us that You are merely delaying the cruel and never unjust, even by the weight of a single atom. Make us witnesses to Your justice in this life, and part of its fulfillment in the next. And let us love justice ourselves and be purified from oppression within. Restore to the victims what was stolen from them, and to the weary hearts what hope they lost. Make us a people who act with equity and never swerve from accountability.
57. Ya Hakam (The Judge)
Ya Hakam, You are the Judge when the courts of men fail. When truth is buried beneath lies, let Your verdict rise clear and unstoppable. Judge between us and those who wrong us without remorse, and let us not find ourselves except on the side of the oppressed.
58. Ya Hasib (The Reckoner)
Ya Hasib, You are enough when proof is hidden and power is abused. Account for every tear that fell unseen and every cruelty that was silenced by fear. Let Your reckoning come swift for those who harm, and ever so gently for those who endured.
59. Ya Razzaq (The Provider)
Ya Razzaq, You provide for every creature in ways we cannot see. Feed my heart before my hands, and let my trust in Your sustenance outweigh my fear of the future. Provide for me in ways that bring me closer to You, and never let what I earn make me forget who it's from.
60. Ya Karim (The Most Generous)
Ya Karim, You give beyond what I deserve, and with a dignity too noble for me to live up to. Let Your generosity flow through me as a reflection of how it flows from You. Make my giving noble and my gratitude constant. When You withhold, remind me that You are still giving, even if in ways I may not yet understand.
61. Ya Akram (The Most Bountiful)
Ya Akram, the most generous of those who give, honor me with contentment in every decree. Make my share of this world enough, and my portion of the next abundant. Let me live in gratitude for Your generosity that I can never live up to, and die with praise for a Lord who always gave more.
62. Ya Qawiyy (The Strong)
Ya Qawiyy, strengthen me where I am weak, and make me firm where I bend too easily. Lift me so that I can lift others without ever believing I lifted myself. When the weight of the world feels heavier than my faith, remind me that Your strength is the only way to see through it. Let me draw strength from Your power, but never be weakened by my pride.
63. Ya Matin (The Firm)
Ya Matin, make me steady when everything else shakes. Let my faith be anchored deep enough so that no loss can uproot it. Keep my footing secure in moments that test my balance.
64. Ya Mu'izz (The Bestower of Honor)
Ya Mu'izz, grant me the honor that comes only from You. Let my dignity rest in humility before You, my worth be measured only in worship of You. Protect me from chasing validation from those as fragile as I, and make my highest victory the achievement of Your pleasure.
65. Ya Shafi (The Healer)
Ya Shafi, heal me in ways that medicine can't fix. Remove whatever sickness has touched my body, and expel along with it any sin that poisoned my soul. Let every ache remind me of Your mercy, and every cure draw me closer to You. Heal this Ummah from its wounds, and make us whole again through faith and patience.
66. Ya Tayyib (The Good / Pure)
Ya Tayyib, make my life pure enough to be accepted by You. Purify my wealth from the unlawful and my worship from showing off. Purify my clothes and my food in every way that I seek You.
67. Ya Mu'ti (The Giver)
Ya Mu'ti, Giver of all that heals and all that sustains, let me never forget that every gift from You is a blessed call back to You, and let it find me only as a grateful recipient and servant.
68. Ya Sabur (The Patient)
Ya Sabur, teach me the patience that doesn't expire. Make me calm when delay tests me, and grateful even as I wait. Grant me patience over my desires, and through my trials, and upon the deeds that will protect me from the Fire.
69. Ya Haleem (The Forbearing)
Ya Haleem, You see my mistakes and still give me time to fix them. You watch my slips and still cover them in kindness. Make me forbearing with those around me, and quick to forgiveness with those who may wrong me.
70. Ya Aliyy (The Most High)
Ya Aliyy, raise my heart above resentment and smallness. Lift my gaze towards the higher ways that reach You, and place my hopes in what lasts with You. Let my character rise even when my status falls, for You are higher than everything that confuses me or defeats me. Lift me above pettiness, above despair, above sin, until my soul finds its peace in Your elevation.
71. Ya Kabir (The Great)
Ya Kabir, how small I am before You. Let humility settle where arrogance once lived. Remind me that greatness belongs only to You and can only be realized through You. Shut the doors to what diminishes me, and guide my devotion to what fills me through You.
72. Ya Azim (The Magnificent)
Ya Azim, my Lord of might without measure, make my reverence for You heavier than any fear of creation. Let my heart bow before You long before my body does, and let my most unstable positions be stilled by my trust in You.
73. Ya Muta'ali (The Supremely Exalted)
Ya Muta'ali, You are higher than every desire I chase. Lift me from the illusions of this world, and make my ambitions worthy of Your nearness. Let my soul rise to You with sincerity and longing until I meet You in the highest of places.
74. Ya Majeed (The Glorious)
Ya Majeed, the Glorious whose greatness humbles all glory, honor me by keeping me close. Let my meeting with You there be an extension of my meetings with You here. Make my remembrance of You my greatest title, and my servitude to You my highest rank.
75. Ya Jawad (The Generous)
Ya Jawad, Your generosity reaches me before I even raise my hands. You give before I ask, and more than I deserve. Let me never mistake Your gifts for my earning. Make my giving a small reflection of Yours—open-handed, unseen, and without expectation.
76. Ya Mannan (The Bestower of Favors)
Ya Mannan, every blessing I have is a favor You began. You wrote kindness into my story long before I could thank You. Let me recognize Your favors not as entitlement, but as invitation.
77. Ya Wahhab (The Supreme Bestower)
Ya Wahhab, the Giver of gifts without reason or limit, grant me what only love can grant: forgiveness without condition, and openings without explanation. Gift me faith that doesn't falter when I fall, and hope that keeps finding You even when I can't see the way.
78. Ya Afuww (The Pardoner)
Ya Afuww, erase what I've done in the mercy that only You can write. Wipe away the traces of every sin that followed me into this night, until I stand before You as if I'd never fallen. Let my shame become the soil from which sincerity grows, and my repentance the rope that pulls me back to You. You love to pardon, so pardon me completely. Forgive what I remember and what I've forgotten, what I've confessed and what I've concealed. Make my record as clean as the day You created me, and my heart lighter than it has ever been before. Let me walk out of this night free of what once chained me, hopeful in what awaits me, and humbled by the Lord who forgives simply because He loves to.
79. Ya Hayyiyy (The Modest / Shy One)
Ya Hayyiyy, You are too shy to turn away these hands. How many times have I asked with nothing worthy in return, and yet You still answered in a way that left me in awe? Forgive me for my mountains of fault for just a droplet of Your mercy, and give me more than I dare to ask because of Your generosity.
80. Ya Jami' (The Gatherer)
Ya Jami', gather the pieces of my heart that this world has broken, or perhaps I myself have broken. Unite what distance and time have separated. Bring together my past and my purpose, my loved ones and the reward that You've promised, until we meet again under the shade of Your throne.
81. Ya Warith (The Inheritor)
Ya Warith, as everything returns to You, what fades for me remains preserved with You. Keep my deeds when I am gone and let them grow, and my legacy when I'm forgotten, so long as not by You. Inherit my soul with Your pleasure, and leave behind only what You'll love to return to me for when I meet You.
82. Ya Awwal (The First)
Ya Awwal, You were there before beginnings were written. Before my first breath, You already knew my name. Remind me that nothing starts without You, and that every good I begin is only by Your permission. Let my first step in anything always be toward You.
83. Ya Akhir (The Last)
Ya Akhir, You remain when all else fades. When every name is forgotten, Yours endures. Let my last breath be in Your remembrance, and my last sight be upon Your mercy.
84. Ya Zahir (The Manifest)
Ya Zahir, You are evident in every beauty, in the rise of the sun and the appearance of the moon. Let me see Your majesty in all that surrounds me, until everything I witness becomes a door back to You.
85. Ya Batin (The Hidden / Intimate)
Ya Batin, You are nearer than my thoughts, closer than my pulse. You know the things I can't say, and forgive the things I can't hide. Preserve me in the unseen as You do in the open, and let my nearness to You outlast my time on this earth.
86. Ya Allah (The One True God)
Ya Allah, when I forget even myself, let Your name always remain in my heart and on my tongue. And let my heart always find its way back to You. When I call You by this Name, I am calling every mercy, every promise, every door that leads home. You are what no one else is, and for me what no one else can be.
87. Ya Hayy (The Ever-Living)
Ya Hayy, the Ever-Living who never fades, put life into my worship when it starts to fade, and life into my purpose when I start to feel lost again. Let my faith in You stay alive when the world floods me with dying promises, and the devil deceives with his deception.
88. Ya Qayyum (The Sustainer)
Ya Qayyum, the Sustainer of all that exists, hold me together when I fall apart. Sustain my heart with Your remembrance and my deeds with sincerity. Do not leave me to myself, even for the blink of an eye. Steady me when I swerve with Your guidance. Receive me when I return with Your grace.
89. Ya Allah (The Most Merciful)
Ya Allah, when I rise from one of Your seasons of incredible mercy, let me remember that You were always merciful from the beginning, and You will always be in the end, so long as I return to You. Ya Allah, return me to You. Ya Allah, return me to You. Ya Allah, return me to You, in the presence of Your beloved (Sallallahu Alayhi Wasallam) and all those who knew You the way I want to. In the beginning, and the end, and always to You, all praise is due.
90. Ya Rahim (The Bestower of Mercy)
Ya Rahim, wrap me in Your special mercy reserved for the believers. When my deeds fall short and my heart grows heavy, let Your compassion carry me through. Forgive my daily slips and embrace me with the tenderness that only You can provide.
91. Ya Muhaymin (The Guardian)
Ya Muhaymin, stand guard over my vulnerable heart. Protect my faith from the whispers of doubt, and my soul from the unseen harms of this world. Keep my internal state pure under Your watchful care, long after I have let my own guard down.
92. Ya Mutakabbir (The Supreme/Majestic)
Ya Mutakabbir, rid my heart of its own arrogance and pride. Remind me of my absolute smallness before Your supreme majesty, and let me find true honor in lowering myself entirely to You. Protect me from ever looking down on another soul.
93. Ya Bari' (The Maker/Evolver)
Ya Bari', You evolve everything from nothingness to its perfect state. Take my brokenness and mold it into something beautiful. Cure my soul of its deepest spiritual ailments, and reform me into the servant You created me to be.
94. Ya Qabid (The Restricter)
Ya Qabid, when You restrict my provisions or my paths, let me know it is only to protect me. Squeeze my heart only to expel the love of this fleeting world from it, and let every restriction be a redirection to what is truly better for me.
95. Ya Basit (The Extender)
Ya Basit, expand my chest to gracefully receive Your decree. Expand my provision so that it may be a means of immense charity, and extend my lifespan only if it allows me to gather more deeds that bring me closer to You.
96. Ya Khafid (The Abaser)
Ya Khafid, lower the ranks of the oppressors who spread corruption on this earth. Lower my own ego so that I never act arrogantly over anyone else, and let me be humbled in this life so that I am not humiliated in the next.
97. Ya Mudhill (The Dishonorer/Humiliator)
Ya Mudhill, humiliate the falsehood that masquerades as truth. Protect me from ever being disgraced in this life or the Hereafter, and never let my private sins become the source of my own public humiliation.
98. Ya Ghafur (The Much-Forgiving)
Ya Ghafur, forgive the sins I keep repeating out of my own weakness. Blanket my overwhelming shortcomings with Your endless pardon, and let me never despair of Your immense forgiveness, no matter how far I have strayed.
99. Ya Muqit (The Nourisher)
Ya Muqit, nourish my soul with deep faith just as You nourish my body with daily provision. Sustain my devotion in times of spiritual starvation, and let me never hunger or thirst for anything that displeases You.
100. Ya Ba'ith (The Resurrector)
Ya Ba'ith, revive my dead heart just as You will raise the dead from their graves. Awaken my conscience before my time runs out, and resurrect my soul on the Day of Judgment among those You love most.
101. Ya Muhsi (The Accounter)
Ya Muhsi, You keep count of every breath I take and every leaf that falls. Let my counted deeds be ones of sincere worship, and by Your ultimate grace, overlook the countless mistakes and idle moments I have accumulated.
102. Ya Mu'id (The Restorer)
Ya Mu'id, restore to me the innocence and sweetness of my early faith. Whenever I wander far away, bring me back to Your path gently. Restore my hope when everything around me feels completely lost.
103. Ya Muhyi (The Giver of Life)
Ya Muhyi, breathe life into my good intentions before they wither away. Give life to our broken Ummah, and let my heart beat vibrantly with nothing but deep love and longing for You.
104. Ya Mumit (The Bringer of Death)
Ya Mumit, let me die upon Islam and sincere faith. When my appointed time arrives, let my departure be peaceful, my final words be the Shahada, and let my grave become a spacious garden from the gardens of Paradise.
105. Ya Wajid (The Finder)
Ya Wajid, You possess all things and lack absolutely nothing. Help me find my way when I am utterly lost, and let me find all my comfort, richness, and belonging in You alone.
106. Ya Majid (The Noble)
Ya Majid, elevate my character with true nobility. Let the brilliance of Your glory outshine my base desires, and make me generous, dignified, and noble in my daily dealings with all of Your creation.
107. Ya Ahad (The Unique)
Ya Ahad, You are uniquely One, without any partner, equivalent, or peer. Purify my intentions so that they are uniquely for You alone, completely unshared with the desire for anyone else's praise or validation.
108. Ya Muqtadir (The Determiner/All-Powerful)
Ya Muqtadir, You hold the ultimate measure of all things. When I feel entirely powerless against my circumstances, anchor my heart to Your absolute capability. Decree for me a beautiful destiny that I cannot yet see.
109. Ya Barr (The Source of All Goodness)
Ya Barr, rain down Your pure goodness upon my life. Make me dutiful to my parents, deeply kind to Your creation, and let every single goodness I receive become a bridge that leads me straight to Your pleasure.
110. Ya Muntaqim (The Avenger)
Ya Muntaqim, avenge the innocent and the oppressed who cry out to You in the dead of the night. Bring justice upon the tyrants who have no fear of You, and protect me from ever being the oppressor or the cause of someone else's pain.
111. Ya Ra'uf (The Compassionate)
Ya Ra'uf, Your compassion is softer and warmer than a mother's embrace. Deal with my profound flaws with Your ultimate tenderness, and cushion my many falls with Your endless, gentle grace.
112. Ya Malik-ul-Mulk (Master of the Kingdom)
Ya Malik-ul-Mulk, You give sovereignty to whom You will and strip it from whom You will. Make me a loyal, humble servant in Your vast kingdom, and strip away any false sense of control I stubbornly cling to in my life.
113. Ya Dhu-al-Jalal wa-al-Ikram (Lord of Majesty and Generosity)
Ya Dhu-al-Jalal wa-al-Ikram, shower me relentlessly with Your generosity, and humble me entirely with Your majesty. By Your grace, make this face of mine one that is blessed to look upon Yours in the Hereafter.
114. Ya Mughni (The Enricher)
Ya Mughni, enrich my soul with absolute contentment. Free me from the exhausting poverty of greed and comparison, and make me the wealthiest of people in my complete reliance on You alone.
115. Ya Mani' (The Preventer/Withholder)
Ya Mani', withhold from me anything—no matter how much I desire it—that will draw me away from You. Prevent harm from reaching my family, and strongly block the pathways of sin before my feet can even step onto them.
116. Ya Darr (The Creator of the Harmful)
Ya Darr, no harm can ever touch me except by Your decree. Protect me from the trials and harms of this world, and let any distress I am forced to endure be an ultimate purification and elevation for my soul.
117. Ya Nafi' (The Creator of Good / The Propitious)
Ya Nafi', bring ultimate benefit to my life and my Hereafter through the knowledge I seek and the deeds I perform. Make my presence on this earth a profound source of benefit and light to everyone I meet.
118. Ya Baqi (The Everlasting)
Ya Baqi, when everything I love in this fleeting, fragile world eventually perishes, let my connection to You passionately remain. Make my righteous deeds everlasting in my record, and grant me an eternal, everlasting dwelling in Your highest Paradise.
